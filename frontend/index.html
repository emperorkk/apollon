Source material

Every headline, summary, and article record shown here originates from a publicly available RSS feed that the publisher itself voluntarily makes available for syndication. Every article links directly back to its original publisher, and readers are encouraged to visit the source for the full story.

Copyright basis (EU)

Directive (EU) 2019/790 (the "DSM Directive"), Article 15, grants press publishers rights over reproduction of press publications by online services, but explicitly excludes hyperlinking and the use of individual words or "very short extracts" (Art. 15(1), 15(3)). Per the Court of Justice of the EU (Svensson, C-466/12; GS Media, C-160/15), hyperlinking to content a rightsholder has made freely and lawfully available online does not itself constitute a "communication to the public" requiring separate authorisation. Article 15(3) also preserves the existing quotation and other exceptions under Directive 2001/29/EC.

Important caveats

What counts as a "very short extract" under Article 15 is not harmonised across EU member states, and this remains a genuinely unsettled area of law. Many sources aggregated here are headquartered outside the EU and are governed by their own national copyright frameworks, not EU law. This notice is provided for transparency only and does not constitute legal advice.
